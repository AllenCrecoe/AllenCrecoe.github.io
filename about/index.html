Who is Karl Marx?

&ensp;&ensp;Karl Heinrich Marx was a German philosopher, critic of political economy, economist, historian, sociologist, political theorist, journalist and socialist revolutionary. Marx is typically cited as one of the principal architects of modern social science. He has been described as one of the most influential figures in human history. The best-known titles of him are The Communist Manifesto and the three-volume Das Kapital. Many intellectuals, labour unions, artists, and political parties worldwide have been influenced by Marx’s work, with many modifying or adapting his ideas.

What is Communism?

&ensp;&ensp;Communism is a far-left philosophical, social, political, and economic ideology and movement whose goal is the establishment of a communist society, namely a socioeconomic order structured upon the ideas of common or social ownership of the means of production, as opposed to private ownership, and can involve the absence of social classes, money, and the state.

&ensp;&ensp;Communist political thought and theory are diverse but share several core elements. The dominant forms of communism are based on Marxism or Leninism. But non-Marxist versions of communism, such as anarcho-communism and Christian communism, which remain partly influenced by Marxist theories and libertarian and humanist Marxism in particular, also exist.

Why is Manifesto of the Communist Party so important?

&ensp;&ensp;Manifesto of the Communist Party formed the basis for the modern communist movement, arguing that capitalism would inevitably self-destruct, to be replaced by socialism and ultimately communism.
